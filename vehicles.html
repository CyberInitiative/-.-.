The updated version of the compact hatchback from BMW has received a number of changes compared to previous modifications, especially with regard to the power plant. In general, the electric car has retained its inherent style and driving performance.

Performance BMW i3 2019

The 2019 version of the model received a new rechargeable battery with a capacity of 42.2 kWh (120 Ah). Now the energy consumption of the BMW i3 electric car in the combined cycle is 13.1 kWh per 100 km. High engine efficiency and increased battery capacity have led to the fact that the range of the updated car is now 30% more than its predecessors and is 310 km according to the WLTP standard.


Charging an BMW i3 electric car with a 42.2 kWh battery:

As before, the BMW i3 can be charged:

  • from a household outlet (2.4 kW, 10 A, 240 V) - 15 hours;
  • using the BMW i Wallbox (3.7 kW, 16 A, 240 V) - 9 hours;
  • from a three-phase network and BMW i Wallbox (11 kW, 16 A, 380 V) - 3 hours;
  • at fast charging stations with a capacity of 50 kW in 45 minutes, up to 80% of the battery capacity.

Chassis of the new BMW i3 2019 model year:

A key update to the model is a 10 mm lowered sports suspension, which includes special shock absorbers, springs and stabilizers.


Options BMW i3

The choice of body finishes for the BMW i3 has been expanded to include the new Jucaro Beige metallic and Frozen Gray.

Loft's optional interior design is available to match the new exterior trim.


Also, the electric car received new 20-inch alloy wheels Jet Black or Bicolor.

Adaptive LED optics with an automatic function of selective inclusion of a high beam is now offered optionally to standard LED headlights. The latest matrix technology of optics has increased the range of lighting, eliminating the blinding effect for vehicles moving towards them. The innovation of the technology is to divide the headlights into four horizontal segments, which can be activated and deactivated independently from each other at a speed of more than 70 km / h.

New BMW i3: stylistic accents in design

The evolution of the i3's exterior design not only brings to the forefront the sporting features of the new product, but also emphasizes its sophisticated elegance. The sharp protrusions and contours of the body with contrasting colors add expressiveness to the appearance of the electric car.

Performance BMW i3 2018

i3 will be equipped with a 125 kW / 170 hp synchronous motor. with a torque of 250 Nm, which accelerates the car from 0 to 100 km in just 7.3 seconds with a maximum speed of 150 km / h (limited by electronics).

The i3 is powered by linear-ion high-voltage outdoor batteries, providing a range of 290 to 300 kilometers on the NEDC cycle, from 235 to 255 kilometers in accordance with WLTP and up to 200 kilometers in everyday use.

New BMW i3s: dynamic design

The new BMW i3s includes many design elements that emphasize its particularly dynamic character. Both front and rear bumpers feature powerful contours and a U-shaped contrast trim. The side sections of the front bumper are edged with accent strips of BMW i Blue or Frozen Gray, which further emphasizes the width of the car. The i3s grille is painted in glossy black to look larger and more expressive. The rear bumper of the new BMW i3s also has clear contours with color trim.

The BMW i3s comes standard with sports suspension and exclusive 20-inch twin-spoke alloy wheels, which are 20 mm wider than previously available versions. They can be ordered in Bicolour and Jet Black.

Technical features of the BMW i3s

The i3s has a high-performance electric motor rated at 135 kW / 184 hp, which generates a maximum torque of 270 Nm. Its drive system includes modified engine management and special tapered roller bearings, which are used to optimize power supply and performance at higher speeds.

The BMW i3s also boasts sports suspension with specially designed springs, shock absorbers and anti-roll bars. Drivers can select SPORT mode using the drive control switch. This leads to a more direct response of the accelerator and more stringent steering characteristics, to further emphasize the sportiness of the car.

The BMW i3s accelerates from 0 to 100 km in just 6.9 seconds, developing a top speed of 160 km / h (electronically limited). The car has a combined power consumption of 14.3 kWh per 100 kilometers in the NEDC cycle. Its range is 280 kilometers on the NEDC cycle, from 235 to 245 km in accordance with WLTP and up to 200 kilometers in everyday use.
